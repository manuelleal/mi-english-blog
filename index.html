Letter to a Friend

Letter to a Friend:

Hi, Fred!

It's been a while since we have been in touch. How has your semester been?

I wanted to send an email update to you to let you know how things have been going during my semester abroad in Málaga, Spain. I've been here for six weeks and I feel like I'm finally adapting to the culture. I'm also speaking the language more fluently.

I arrived during the first week of September. The weather has been very nice – even though it's October, it's still sunny and warm. In fact, I went to the beach and swam in the Mediterranean Sea earlier today.

I'm living with a very welcoming host family. I have my own private bedroom, but we eat together for all meals. On Sundays, we enjoy a big home-cooked paella for lunch. In Spain, lunch is usually the biggest meal of the day and it's common to take a nap after.

On weekdays, I attend classes at a local university where I've met several native Spanish speakers. They have been kind and patient with me, and my understanding has greatly improved.

I'm so glad I decided to spend the semester here. We even have an extended weekend coming up, and my friends and I plan to travel to France for a few days. I look forward to hearing from you soon.

Best wishes,
Patrick

Questions:

  • In which country is Patrick studying?
  • What meal is described as the biggest of the day in Spain?

Source: Personal letter
